Our Origin

Hibydates was founded in 2026 with a singular vision: to unlock the immense agricultural potential of Nigeria's hibiscus and date palm sectors while creating sustainable livelihoods for smallholder farmers. We operate at the intersection of tradition and innovation, leveraging generations of farming expertise with modern export standards.

Our name reflects our dual specialization — Hibiscus and Dates — two crops that thrive in Nigeria's unique agro-ecological zones, particularly in the northern states of Kano, Jigawa, and Bauchi.

Our Mission: To become Nigeria's most trusted agricultural export partner by delivering premium quality products that meet international standards while ensuring farmer prosperity.

Farm Sourcing

We maintain direct partnerships with registered farming cooperatives in Kano, Jigawa, and Bauchi states. Our agronomists provide pre-harvest guidance to ensure crops meet international standards from the field.

Quality Assurance

Every batch undergoes rigorous testing for moisture content, foreign matter, microbiological safety, and chemical residues. We exceed Nigerian SON standards and meet EU, US, and Middle Eastern import requirements.

Export Logistics

Why Nigerian Hibiscus?

Nigerian hibiscus, particularly the Kano Red variety, is globally recognized for its superior quality, deep crimson color, and high hibiscus acid content. Unlike Egyptian or Sudanese varieties, Nigerian hibiscus offers:

  • Higher anthocyanin content (vivid color)
  • Stronger floral aroma profile
  • Better brewing characteristics
  • Preferred by European tea blenders
  • Clean pesticide profile potential

Applications

Tea & Beverage: Herbal infusions, wellness teas, functional beverages
Food: Natural coloring, flavoring, confectionery
Pharmaceutical: Herbal supplements, antioxidant extracts
Cosmetics: Natural dyes, skincare formulations

Nigerian Date Palm Fruits

Phoenix dactylifera — Cultivated in Nigeria's semi-arid zones

  • Origin: Jigawa, Bauchi, Yobe States
  • Varieties: Deblit Noor, Medjool, local premium
  • Moisture: Max 18% (Dried Grade A)
  • Sugar Content: Min 70%
  • Packaging: 5kg/10kg cartons, 20' reefer containers
  • Availability: June - September (Peak Season)

Nigeria's Date Potential

Nigeria's date palm sector is experiencing rapid growth, with the government targeting ₦300 billion annually from date exports. Our northern states offer ideal growing conditions with increasing yields and improving quality.

Processing Excellence

We employ solar drying techniques and modern sorting to ensure uniform size, optimal moisture, and extended shelf life. Our dates meet Middle Eastern quality standards while offering competitive African pricing.

Market Applications

Food Industry: Natural sweetener, bakery, confectionery
Retail: Premium snacking, gift packaging
Health: Energy bars, natural supplements
Industrial: Date paste, syrup production

Port Operations

We operate primarily through Apapa Port, Lagos — Nigeria's premier seaport and the gateway for agricultural exports. Recent port modernization has significantly improved efficiency, with dedicated agricultural export terminals and faster customs processing.

Shipping Capabilities

  • 20' Standard containers (15MT hibiscus)
  • 20' Reefer containers (dates)
  • LCL (Less Container Load) consolidation
